Cold Call Script

Opening (Neutral, Local, Non-Salesy)

You: "Hey, is this [Seller Name]?"
Seller: "Yeah, who's this?"
You: "My name's [Your Name], I work with a fix and flip company here locally. I was calling about the property on [Street Name]. I was in the area, came across it, and wanted to see if you'd ever thought about selling it, or if you'd be open to a cash offer."

(Pause. Let them respond.)

If They Say "NO" or "NOT REALLY"

You: "Gotcha, totally understand. Out of curiosity though, if someone ever made you an offer that made sense, would you at least hear it out?"

If still no ‚Üí exit politely. If yes ‚Üí continue.

If They Say "MAYBE / POSSIBLY / YEAH"

You: "Okay, cool. I appreciate you being open. Just so I'm not wasting your time, can I ask you a couple quick questions about it?"

Qualification Questions

Motivation

You: "What's the main reason you'd even consider selling it?"
(Let them talk. Do not interrupt.)

Timing

You: "If you did sell it, when would you ideally want that to happen?"

Condition

You: "How's the property overall? Would you say it's livable as-is, or does it need some work?"

If needs work: "What kind of stuff are we talking about? Roof, kitchen, bathrooms, or mostly cosmetic?"

If it's fine: "Any big-ticket items replaced recently? Roof, HVAC, plumbing?"

Price Discovery

You: "If you decided to sell it, do you have a ballpark number in mind?"

If they give a price: (Pause 2 seconds) "Okay... I appreciate you sharing that." (No reaction. No negotiating yet.)

If they ask you first: "I usually like to hear what makes sense for you before throwing out a number."

The Close (Soft, Not Desperate)

You: "Based on what you told me, it sounds like something we could make work. What I need to do is run the numbers and see if it makes sense on our end. If it does, would you be open to moving forward?"
FINAL RULES:
  • Don't say "wholesaler"
  • Don't over-explain
  • Don't talk too much
  • Don't chase
  • Stay calm
  • Stay consistent

FB Marketplace DM Flow

Phase 1: The Filter

Step 1: Initial Opener

You: "Hey, I'm very interested. Are you a realtor or the homeowner?"
Why it works: Filters agents instantly, puts you in control, and feels like a natural inquiry.

Step 2: If They're a Realtor/Agent

You: "Got it, appreciate the honesty. I'll pass for now."

Action: End the conversation. Delete. Move on. No explaining or justifying.

Step 3: If They're the Homeowner

You: "Perfect, thanks. Are you selling it yourself or do you have an agent helping you at all?"

Phase 2: Positioning & Motivation

Step 4: If They Have an Agent (But responded to you)

You: "That makes sense. Are you open to a direct cash offer if it made sense, or are you pretty set on going the traditional route?"

Step 5: Soft Context

You: "Reason I ask is I work with a small local fix-and-flip group and we buy properties as-is, no repairs or showings."

Step 6: Permission-Based Motivation Probe

You: "Out of curiosity, what's got you thinking about selling this one?"

If they dodge: "Totally fair. Is it more of a 'just testing the market' situation or are you hoping to sell sooner rather than later?"

Step 7: Timeline Check

You: "If everything lined up, how soon would you want to be done with it?"

Phase 3: The Numbers & Condition

Step 8: Price Discovery

You: "Have you had a price in mind that you'd feel good about, or are you just seeing what kind of offers come in?"

Rule: Let the seller speak first. If they give a number, do not react yet.

Step 9: Condition Snapshot

You: "Got it. How's the property overall? Any major repairs I should know about like roof, foundation, or HVAC?"

Step 10: Soft Reality Anchor

You: "Okay, that helps. Most of the homes we buy need a little work, so our offers usually reflect that, but it keeps things fast and simple."

Phase 4: Negotiation & Objections

Step 12: Ballpark Range

You: "If we were to be somewhere around [Low Number] to [High Number], would that even be worth continuing the conversation?"

Rule: Never say "offer" yet. Use the phrase "somewhere around."

Step 13: If They Say "Too Low"

You: "I get that. I just want to be upfront so we don't waste each other's time. What number would you actually need to make it worth it?"

Step 14: If They Say "I Need to Think"

You: "Totally understand. Why don't you think it over and I'll check back in a couple days?"

Phase 5: Follow-Up Sequence

Day 2: "Hey, just wanted to circle back and see if selling the property is still something you're open to."

Day 5: "Still interested in selling, or did you decide to go a different direction?"

Day 10: "No worries if now's not the right time. If anything changes, feel free to reach out."

Core Rules for Success:
  • Never explain wholesaling
  • Never over-text. Keep messages brief
  • Listen more than you talk
  • Stay calm. Confidence comes from clarity, not pressure
  • Consistency closes deals

Follow-Up System

The Follow-Up Mindset

The deal is never dead until they say "no" twice.

Follow-up is defined by value and consistency, not pressure. Your objective is to:

  • Maintain a presence as a professional option
  • Facilitate a streamlined decision-making process
  • Ensure the seller retains a sense of agency and control

Follow-Up Timeline

Day 0: Initial Contact

Send a text message 30‚Äì90 minutes following the initial conversation.

Text: "Hello [Name], thank you for your time earlier. I am currently reviewing the figures and will reach out once I have a proposal that aligns with the property details."

Day 1

Text: "Hello [Name], following up briefly. Are you still planning to review the details today or tomorrow?"

Day 3

Text: "I wanted to check in and see if any circumstances have changed on your end since our last conversation."

Day 5

Text: "Hello [Name], I am reviewing several properties this week. Should I keep this one on my active list, or would you prefer I check back at a later date?"

Day 7

Text: "Are you still open to a cash offer, or have you decided to pursue a different direction with the property?"

Weekly Follow-Up (Weeks 2‚Äì4)

Transmit one communication per week, rotating through these templates:

  • "Checking in to see if you are still considering a sale, or if I should reach out further down the road."
  • "This property came up in my notes today; I wanted to reach out and see where you stand."
  • "If your timeline or requirements change in the future, please feel free to contact me directly."

Long-Term Follow-Up (30‚Äì90 Days)

Critical: This is where most wholesalers fail, yet where the majority of transactions are finalized.

Contact the seller once every 2‚Äì4 weeks:

Text: "Hello [Name], I hope you are doing well. I wanted to see if there have been any updates regarding the property."

Market Insight Variation: "Market conditions have shifted recently, so the valuation parameters may differ from our previous discussion."

Protocol for Voice Follow-Up

Engagement Triggers: Initiate a phone call if the seller was previously receptive, provided a specific timeline, or remained engaged via text.

Call Script: "Hello [Name], this is [Your Name]. I wanted to reach out briefly to see how things have progressed on your end."

Termination of Follow-Up

Discontinue communication only under these conditions:

  • The seller explicitly states "not interested" on two separate occasions
  • A formal request to stop communication is made
  • The interaction becomes unprofessional or non-constructive
Core Follow-Up Principles:
  • Avoid consecutive messaging: Do not send multiple messages without a response
  • Maintain neutrality: Avoid emotional or defensive language
  • Prioritize brevity: Do not over-explain the process
  • Strategic spacing: Allow time between contacts to maintain professional value
Closing Insight: Sellers often finalize a decision when they feel a sense of security and professional consistency. Frequent, composed follow-up is the primary method for establishing that trust.

Objection Cheat Sheet

Instructions: Use these word-for-words. Speak calmly. Then, remain silent.

1. "I'm talking to other investors"

You: "That makes sense; most sellers I talk to are. I'm not trying to win you over, I just want to see if this is even a fit."

Why it works: Removes competition pressure, positions you as selective, and lowers the seller's guard.

2. "I need to think about it"

You: "Totally fair. What part are you thinking through?"

If they say price: "Got it. Is it more about the number, or the process itself?"

If vague: "No rush at all. Want me to follow up in a couple days, or should I just let you reach out if you decide?"

3. "Your price is too low"

You: "I figured you might say that. Help me understand, what were you hoping to be closer to?"

If they give a number: "Okay, and if we could get close to that, would you actually be ready to move forward?"

4. "I want retail price"

You: "I hear you. Most sellers do. The trade-off with us is speed and simplicity, not top dollar."

(Pause after speaking)

5. "I'm not in a rush"

You: "That's good‚Äîno pressure then. If the right offer came along, would you still consider it?"

6. "I want to list it"

You: "That's a solid option. Some sellers just don't want showings, repairs, or waiting. Just depends what matters more to you."

7. "Why so many questions?"

You: "Fair question. I just don't want to throw out a number that doesn't make sense for either of us."

8. "How do I know you're legit?"

You: "Totally get that. I work with a local fix-and-flip group and we close through a reputable title company. Everything's in writing."

Follow-up: "I can also walk you through the exact process if that helps."

9. "Are you a wholesaler?"

You: "I work with a fix-and-flip company. Sometimes we keep properties, sometimes we partner with other buyers. Either way, we close the same."

10. "I don't want to sign anything yet"

You: "No problem. We wouldn't move forward unless you're comfortable anyway. What would you need to see to feel comfortable?"

11. "This feels too fast"

You: "That's fair. Fast doesn't mean rushed. You're in control the whole time."

12. "I'll call you back"

You: "Sounds good. Do you want me to check back in tomorrow or later this week?"
Golden Rules (Do Not Break):
  • Never argue
  • Never "educate" to convince
  • Never chase energy
  • Calm confidence beats logic
  • Silence is a tool
