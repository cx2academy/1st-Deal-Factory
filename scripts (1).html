Cold Call Script

Opening

You:

"Hey, is this [Seller Name]?"

Seller:

"Yeah, who's this?"

You:

"My name's [Your Name], I work with a fix and flip company here locally. I was calling about the property on [Street Name]. I was in the area, came across it, and wanted to see if you'd ever thought about selling it, or if you'd be open to a cash offer."

(Pause. Let them respond.)

If They Say "NO" or "NOT REALLY"

You:

"Gotcha, totally understand. Out of curiosity though, if someone ever made you an offer that made sense, would you at least hear it out?"

If still no ‚Üí exit politely. If yes ‚Üí continue.

If They Say "MAYBE / POSSIBLY / YEAH"

You:

"Okay, cool. I appreciate you being open. Just so I'm not wasting your time, can I ask you a couple quick questions about it?"

Qualification Questions

Motivation

You:

"What's the main reason you'd even consider selling it?"

(Let them talk. Do not interrupt.)

Timing

You:

"If you did sell it, when would you ideally want that to happen?"

Condition

You:

"How's the property overall? Would you say it's livable as-is, or does it need some work?"

If needs work: "What kind of stuff are we talking about? Roof, kitchen, bathrooms, or mostly cosmetic?"

If it's fine: "Any big-ticket items replaced recently? Roof, HVAC, plumbing?"

Price Discovery

You:

"If you decided to sell it, do you have a ballpark number in mind?"

If they give a price: (Pause 2 seconds) "Okay... I appreciate you sharing that." (No reaction. No negotiating yet.)

If they ask you first: "I usually like to hear what makes sense for you before throwing out a number."

The Close

You:

"Based on what you told me, it sounds like something we could make work. What I need to do is run the numbers and see if it makes sense on our end. If it does, would you be open to moving forward?"

Final Rules
  • Don't say "wholesaler"
  • Don't over-explain
  • Don't talk too much
  • Don't chase
  • Stay calm and consistent
